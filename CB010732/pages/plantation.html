Tea Growing Regions in sri lanka

There are three main tea growing regions in Sri Lanka.The taste, flavour and aroma of teas from each region are influenced by the conditions particular to those regions. Low grown teas, which are subjected to long periods of sunshine, dry and somewhat warm and moist conditions, exhibit a burgundy brown liquor and a malt, heavy note with black leaf appearance. Low Grown Regions

Low grown teas which are teas grown on an elevation between sea level to 600m.

Mid Grown Regions

Mid Grown teas which are teas grown on an elevation from 600m to 1,200m

High Grown Regions

High grown teas which are grown at an elevation above 1,200m.

Nuwara Eliya

According to the elevation of Sri Lanka, high-grown tea is found in the central highlands.Nuwara Eliya is one of the major tea growing districts in Sri Lanka. We can find some of the most relished tea types,such as Orange Pekoe and Broken Orange Pekoe in Nuwara Eliya.

Uda Pussellawa

Uda Pussellawa is also one of the main high-grown tea regions of the country.Due to its location, the climate of the Uda Pussallawa region is quite different.Because of that the tea which grows in Uda Pussallawa is slightly darker compared to the tea found in the Nuwara Eliya region

Kandy

Mid grown tea is produced at 2000 to 4000 ft above the sea level. The hill capital of Kandy is another region for mid-grown tea. We can find bright infusions in Kandy region.

Badulla

Badulla tea region is located in the Uva province. We can find a mellow,smooth taste tea which has a unique flavor only in Badulla, Sri Lanka.
