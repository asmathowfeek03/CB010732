RESURGING CEYLON TEA INDUSTRY after the covid-19 pandamic

Sri Lanka is an island made for tea. In 1867 a scot named James Taylor introduced Tea to the Sri Lankan soil and now it becomes the top agriculture export. Nearly 4% of the country’s land area is covered in tea plantation. The central highlands and the southern inland areas of the island are the main tea grown areas. Country produces tea throughout the year and that is, about 340 million kilograms per year. Also Ceylon tea industry provides direct and indirect employment to nearly 1 million people. Ceylon Tea is more popular as it has a distinct flavor and it because tea leaves are exclusively handpicked mainly according to the two leaves and bud method. A larger portion of Sri Lankan tea is exported as Ceylon Black Tea and country produces Ceylon Green Tea as well. As Ceylon tea industry has the capability to produce good quality products, it maintains the Sri Lankan reputation in the global tea market.

Why Ceylon Tea?

Ceylon tea is popular among tea enthusiasts for its rich flavor and fragrant aroma.It comes from the same plant as other forms of tea and has a similar range of nutrients, despite some variances in taste and antioxidant content.Certain types of Ceylon tea have been linked to impressive health advantages, including improved blood sugar control, lower cholesterol levels, and greater fat burning.

Refreshing You

Today, all tea produced in Sri Lanka is completely ozone-friendly. No other country that produces tea can take pride in having this distinction. A blanket restriction on the use of methyl bromide in procedures like export packaging and transportation is now being planned. Since May 2011, all Ceylon Tea products are allowed to display the new "Ozone Friendly Pure Ceylon Tea" label, indicating that no ozone-depleting compounds were used in its production. When you grab a cup of Ceylon tea, you're not just revitalizing yourself—you're also assisting in the renewal and revitalization of a resource that is vital to all life on Earth.

Try Some Tea Flavors

Tea leaves are separated into different grades, and each and every grade has a different color and intensity. According to that there are several flavors of tea and black tea is the most common kind of tea flavor, you can find in Sri Lanka. It is considered as the cleanest tea in the world. Just like the black tea, Ceylon green tea is pure and clean. Even though it comes from the same kind of plant the process of making green tea is different to black tea.

Ginger Tea Recipe

Ingredients (for 1 cup of tea)


  • One tspn Ceylon Black Tea
  • One tspn of crushed ginger
  • Sugar as per taste
  • One cup of boiling water


Method


  1. Boil water.
  2. Adding a small amount of boiling water to the pot and swirl the water & remove before preparing the tea.
  3. Add the ingredients to a tea pot & pour boiling water into it.
  4. Cover the teapot.
  5. Allow to simmer about 4 minutes

(Instead of sugar, you can add honey or other sweetener.) Serve hot!!
