Ceylon Tea Museum

The Ceylon Tea Museum is situated 3 km south of Kandy and it is located in Hanthana. Ceylon Tea Museum was originally constructed in 1925 , incorporated on 9th of January 1998 and it was formally opened on 1st of December 2001. Back then, The Ceylon Tea Museum is the former Hanthana four storied tea factory. Hanthana tea factory had been abandoned for more than a decade. Then the Sri Lanka Tea Board and the Planters’ Association of Sri Lanka were refurbished in 2001.

At present, the four storey ceylon tea museum building consists as follows.

The ground floor has colonial generators, dryers, fermentation tables, sorting machines, rollers etc. The library and museum are in the second floor. The statues of the two tea pioneers, James Taylor and Thomas Lipton are exhibited at the Museum. The third floor has a shop and the top floor has a restaurant with tea rooms.
